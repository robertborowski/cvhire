10 Team Building Icebreaker Activities

Published: May 30, 2023

Overview

Team building icebreakers are activities or games that help team members get to know each other, build trust, and foster a positive group dynamic. Here are some examples of team building icebreakers:

1. Two Truths and a Lie

Each team member shares three statements about themselves, two of which are true and one that is false. The rest of the team tries to guess which statement is the lie.

2. Human Bingo

Create bingo cards with various statements or descriptions (e.g., "Has traveled to more than three countries," "Speaks more than two languages," etc.). Team members mingle and try to find individuals who match the statements to fill out their bingo card.

3. Deserted Island

Ask each team member to imagine they are stranded on a deserted island and can only bring three items with them. They must share their choices and explain their reasoning, sparking conversations and revealing different perspectives.

4. Marshmallow Challenge

Teams are given spaghetti sticks, tape, string, and a marshmallow. The objective is to build the tallest freestanding structure using only these materials within a time limit. It encourages collaboration, creativity, and problem-solving.

5. Team Trivia

Divide the team into smaller groups and create a trivia game with questions related to general knowledge, the company, or the team itself. It promotes friendly competition while encouraging team members to work together.

6. Personal Shields

Provide each team member with a paper plate and art supplies. They have to create a personal shield by drawing symbols, images, or words that represent their personality, strengths, or values. Afterward, they share their shield and explain the meaning behind their choices.

7. Speed Networking

Set up a speed networking session where team members pair up and have a limited time to introduce themselves, share their interests, and exchange information. After each round, participants rotate to meet someone new.

8. Scavenger Hunt

Organize a scavenger hunt in the office or nearby area. Create a list of items or clues that teams must find within a specified time frame. It encourages collaboration, problem-solving, and team bonding.

9. Jigsaw Puzzle

Provide each team with a jigsaw puzzle and challenge them to complete it within a specific time limit. It promotes teamwork, communication, and problem-solving as team members work together to assemble the puzzle.

10. The Name Game

Each team member thinks of an adjective that starts with the same letter as their first name (e.g., "Creative Caroline," "Enthusiastic Eric"). They introduce themselves using the adjective, and each subsequent person must repeat the previous names and adjectives. It helps with name recall and creates a fun atmosphere.

Conclusion

Remember to consider the preferences and comfort levels of your team members when selecting icebreakers. The goal is to create an inclusive and enjoyable environment where everyone can participate and engage.
