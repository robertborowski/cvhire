Tableau vs Power BI - Best Data Visualization Tool

Microsoft’s Power BI is a unified, scalable platform for self-service and enterprise level business intelligence (BI). Power BI can connect to and visualize virtually any dataset while seamlessly infusing visualizations within other apps.

Tableau is a visual analytics platform and market-leading choice for modern business intelligence, Tableau’s analytics platform makes it easier for people to explore and manage data, and faster to discover and share insights that can change businesses decisions.

Both Power BI and Tableau are great data visualization/business intelligence tools but what are some of their key differences between the two products?

1. Timeline

Tableau was first released in 2005 while Power BI was first released in 2011.

2. Cost

Power BI has the benefit of being owned by Microsoft, therefore overall costs tend to be lower for enterprise products and bundles. Therefore Tableau plans are usually more expensive than Power BI. At the time of writing this an individual Power BI Pro license costs $9.99 per month. While a Tableau Creator license costs $70.00 per month.

3. Speed

With both Tableau and Power BI, you can access and analyze data from multiple sources quickly and easily. This allows you to make informed decisions quickly and accurately, giving you the competitive edge. Tableau is generally faster than Power BI when working with very large interconnected datasets.

4. Community

Tableau and Power BI both have active and supportive online communities where users can ask questions and get answers from other users and experts. Each community offers a wide range of helpful resources, such as tutorials, webinars, and forums, to help users understand and maximize the potential of the platform. Both Tableau and Power BI have dedicated user groups offering support and advice, as well as an array of online resources that can help users find answers to their questions.

5. Data sources

Tableau and Power BI both support a wide range of data sources, including relational databases, spreadsheets, Hadoop, cloud-based data sources, and real-time streaming data. Tableau’s data connectors allow users to easily connect to any data source, while Power BI’s built-in connectors provide users with easy access to a range of popular data sources. Tableau and Power BI both offer the ability to blend and combine data sources, enabling users to combine multiple data sources and create a single view of their data.

6. Programming language support

Tableau supports a wider range of programming languages such as C, C++, Java, Python, R. While Power BI mainly focuses on Data Analysis Expressions (DAX) with less of an emphasis on outside programming languages.
